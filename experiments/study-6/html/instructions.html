Imagine that you are traveling very far away, and meet a new group of people you know nothing about. You are trying to learn about the people, the society, and the language, all at the same time.

Based on your identity, your work, and/or your roles, you are (or want to be) in different kinds of relationships with the people in the society.

In some of the relationships, you have more power/status/influence than the other person, in other relationships you have the same amount of power/status/influence as the other person, and in other relationships you have less power/status/influence than the other person.

Instructions

In this study, you will be interacting with different members of this society in a variety of daily-life social interactions. The people you interact with all know the social rules and norms of the society, and act based on these social norms.

We will tell you about the kind of relationship you are in (or want to be in) with each partner: whether they have more, equal, or less power/status/influence than you.

For each of these interactions, there are two actions that both you and your partner need to choose between. One of the choices benefits you more than your partner, the other choice benefits your partner more than you.

Based on what you choose to do and based on what your partner chooses to do, the social interaction will either occur smoothly ‚ò∫Ô∏è or look socially awkward üò¨.

Instructions

You will interact with 6 total partners. You will interact with each partner twice.

Each time you interact with a partner, we will show you a description of a short scenario and will ask you to choose an action.

The first time you interact with a partner, we will show you what your partner selected, and what happened during the social interaction.

The second time you interact with a partner, we will show you a reminder of your first interaction, and then ask you to choose an action again.

Instructions

You can earn up to 2 points for each interaction:

  • You will receive 2 points if the social interaction occurs smoothly and you benefit more than your partner from the social interaction.
  • You will receive 1 point if the social interaction occurs smoothly and you benefit less than your partner from the social interaction.
  • You will receive 0 points if the social interaction looks awkward.
