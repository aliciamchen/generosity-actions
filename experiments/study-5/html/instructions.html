Imagine that you are traveling very far away, and meet a new group of people you know nothing about. You are trying to learn about the people, the society, and the language, all at the same time.

Based on your identity, your work, and/or your roles, there are people who have more power/status/influence than you, people who have the same amount of power/status/influence as you, and people who have less power/status/influence than you.

Instructions

In this study, you will be interacting with different members of this society in a variety of daily-life social interactions.

For each of these interactions, there are two actions that you need to choose between.

If you and your partner choose different actions, the social interaction will occur smoothly; if both of you choose the same action, you look socially awkward and like you haven't figured out the norms of the society.

Instructions

You will interact with 6 total partners. You will interact with each partner twice.

We will tell you whether each partner has more, equal, or less power/status/influence than you.

Each time you interact with a partner, we will ask you to choose an action. Then, we will show you what your partner selected, and what happened during the social interaction.

You will receive 1 point if the social interaction occurs smoothly (i.e. you and your partner choose different actions), and 0 points if you look socially awkward (i.e. you and your partner choose the same action).

At the end of the study, you will receive a bonus of up to $XXX based on how many points you earned.
